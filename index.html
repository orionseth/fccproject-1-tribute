The Super Nintendo

Best System Ever

The 16-bit Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES), or Super Famicom as it is known in Japan, is regarded by many as the best video game system of all time.

It launched in 1990 in Japan, 1991 in North America and 1992 in Europe, and sold over 49 million units worldwide.

Games Library

Some of the greatest video games ever made were released on the SNES during the 1990s.

Around 717 games were released in North America, 1485 in Japan and 521 in Europe. Many of these games are still widely sought after today and have:

  • great audio and catchy soundtracks
  • colorful graphics
  • fun gameplay
  • classic design

Technical Specification

  • Processor: 16-bit Ricoh @3.58 MHz
  • Memory: 128 KB RAM, 64 KB SRAM for video / audio
  • Video: 8 mode Picture Processing Unit, 15-bit color
  • Audio: Sony S-SMP soundchip
  • Media type: ROM cartridge
  • Controller Ports: 2

Lifecycle End

Games development continued in Japan for the Super Famicom well into the 90s, and the last game was released in late 2000.

Producition of the Super Famicom console was discontinued in 2003, when Nintendo ran out of parts to manufacture the system.
