Retro Gaming: Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES)

Best System Ever

The 16-bit Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES), or "Super Famicom" as it is known in Japan, is regarded by many as the best video game system of all time. The SNES was developed by Nintendo and released in 1990 in Japan, 1991 in North America and 1992 in Europe. It sold over 49 million units worldwide.

Games Library

Some of the greatest video games ever made were released on the SNES during the 1990s. Around 717 games were released in North America, 1485 in Japan and 521 in Europe. Although somewhat outdated by today's high-tech standards, these games are still widely sought after today and have:

  • great audio and catchy soundtracks
  • colorful graphics and rich visuals
  • fun addictive gameplay
  • classic look and feel

Technical Specification

  • Media type: ROM cartridge
  • Processor: 16-bit Ricoh 5A22 @3.58 MHz
  • Memory: 128 KB RAM, with 64 KB SRAM for video and audio
  • Video: 8 mode Picture Processing Unit (PPU), 15-bit RGB palette
  • Audio: Sony S-SMP soundchip
  • Controller Ports: 2

Lifecycle End

Games development continued in Japan for the Super Famicom well into the late 90s. The last Super Famicom game made, Metal Slader Glory - Director's Cut, was released in Japan in late 2000. Producition of the Super Famicom console was discontinued several years later in 2003, when Nintendo ran out of parts to manufacture the system.
